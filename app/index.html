Heatscape - Thermal Sensor AI

Role: Product Designer and Front End Developer | Tools: Figma (Pro Version), Jotform, C++, ESP32 Microcontroller, Temperature & Humidity Sensor, Bluetooth Low Energy, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Otter AI, Perplexity AI, Google Bard, GPT-4

Heatscape uses AI and thermal sensors to address climate change impacts in Sydney. My role involved extensive user research, innovative interface design, and iterative prototyping.

Challenge

Addressing the Urban Heat Island effect to improve urban habitability and climate resilience in Sydney.

Goal

These findings align with the priorities of the United Nations‚Äô Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 11, specifically target 11.b, which advocates for the mitigation of and adaptation to climate change impacts in urban areas. (Movement and Place - NSW Government, 2023)

The Sendai Framework for Disaster Risk Reduction 2015-2030 further mandates the importance of understanding and strengthening resilience against environmental hazards like heat waves, often labeled as 'silent disasters.' (ArcGIS StoryMaps, 2021)

Process Overview

Adopted the double-diamond approach encompassing discovery, definition, development, and delivery phases, enriched with iterative design and constant user feedback.

Key Findings

Uncovered a critical connection between UHI effects and socio-economic factors, emphasizing the need for widespread awareness and user-centric solutions.

Prototype and User Testing

Conducted multiple rounds of prototyping and user testing to refine the web application.

Outcome

Developed a responsive web application that leverages real-time body temperature, location and weather for proactive heat mitigation suggestions through AI Chat and maps.

Impact and Reflection

The project not only addresses a critical environmental challenge but also showcases my ability to blend technical skills with empathetic design. It underscores my commitment to sustainable, data-driven solutions.
