Heatscape - Urban Heat Island Effect Solution

Discover: Primary & Secondary Research

The process began with secondary research to identify potential problem areas, followed by primary research including qualitative observations, ethnographic studies, and interviews in Sydney. Quantitative surveys were conducted to gauge public knowledge and habits during high-heat seasons.

Define: Thematic Analysis

The primary research was synthesized into key themes, revealing issues like the relation of heat to wealth, lack of public awareness, and the scarcity of free, cool spaces.

Develop: Ideation & Prototyping

Storyboards were used in the ideation process, and affinity diagramming and thematic analysis helped identify root issues for the prototype. Six iterations were conducted, each refining the core features and interface based on feedback.

Deliver: Final Prototyping & Testing

High-fidelity prototypes were developed and tested, leading to significant enhancements based on user feedback. The final design was developed into a web application, incorporating different APIs for an interactive user experience.

Final Design Elements

The final design features interactive elements like real-time body temperature visualization, simplified navigation, and personalized user experience.

Foodpile - Virtual Pantry and Shopping Lists

Background Research and Problem Space

This stage involved analyzing global food waste trends. According to the United Nations, around one-third of all food produced is wasted. Understanding this helped us define Foodpile's mission to combat household food wastage.

Problem Statement

We articulated the problem of excessive food wastage in households, especially among young adults. This stage was crucial to align our solution with specific user challenges and needs.

User Research Methods

Utilizing surveys and interviews, we gathered data on shopping habits and food usage. This user-centric approach ensured that Foodpile was developed based on real user needs and behaviors.

Affinity Diagram

The affinity diagram helped organize and interpret data from our user research. This visual representation was key in identifying patterns and insights for a user-focused design strategy.

User Persona

Developing a user persona, like 'Justin,' a young adult with a busy lifestyle, helped in tailoring Foodpile's features to meet real user needs effectively.

SDG Goal and Target

Aligning with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals, specifically SDG 12 (Responsible Consumption and Production), highlighted Foodpile‚Äôs commitment to sustainability.

The Solution and Features

Foodpile was designed with features such as smart shopping lists, expiry notifications, and recipe suggestions to minimize waste and encourage responsible consumption.

Existing Solutions and Design Precedents

Analyzing existing solutions provided insights into the market gap and opportunities for innovation, leading to a unique positioning of Foodpile in the market.

Value Proposition

Foodpile‚Äôs value proposition focuses on saving money, reducing waste, and promoting a sustainable lifestyle, addressing key user motivations and market demands.

The Freemium Model

Adopting a freemium model provided a viable business strategy, allowing user growth and scalability while offering premium features for enhanced user experience.

Growth Strategy

Our growth strategy included partnerships with retailers and food waste advocacy groups, leveraging both B2C and B2B channels to maximize reach and impact.

Growth and Retention Rates

Projected growth and retention rates were based on market analysis and user feedback, indicating strong potential for widespread adoption and long-term user engagement.
